mailboxes of the world: Vatican City

Apr 26, 2014 • rachel

Vatican City is its own sovereign country walled off inside of Rome. It is the smallest country in the world and it is an absolute monarchy, with the Pope in charge! Poste Vaticane has operated its own postal service and issued its own postage stamps since 1929. The Vatican mailboxes are very similar to Italy’s, but yellow instead of red. However, you cannot use Italian stamps in the Vatican, or vice versa.

The Vatican Post Office was my first stop after touring the Vatican Museums and St Peter’s Basilica last week. It is set up for tourists, with lots of postcards, souvenirs, and stamps to buy. I sent five postcards from the Vatican. Supposedly, the Vatican postal service is exceptionally reliable, so I hope they all arrive!!

I hear that stamps and mail from the Vatican is quite collectible. I wish I had sent myself a postcard!!

mailboxes of the world: Venice, Italy

Apr 25, 2014 • rachel

The mailboxes in Italy are all about the same, red Poste Italiane boxes, like this one seen in Venice. Many of the mailboxes have graffiti on them, but otherwise, they are nearly identical.

I have mailed quite a few postcards from Italy. But, you guys!! International postcards sent from Italy cost $2 Euro each in postage! Wow. I did not realize it cost almost three times the cost of international postcards from the US. Oh well, snail mail must go on! I bought a LOT of Italian stamps at the Venice post office, like the ones seen below.

I have been taking a photo of every mailbox I see, but many of them in Italy are very similar…I’ll be sure to keep you posted on any unique ones I come across!

Happy snail mailing!!
